# Credit Risk Prediction Model

## Overview

This project demonstrates how predictive analytics can be used to estimate the probability of borrower default using logistic regression. The model analyzes historical borrower characteristics to classify credit applications as low or high risk.

The project reflects the analytical techniques commonly used by banks and financial institutions to support lending decisions and portfolio risk management.

---

## Business Problem

Financial institutions must evaluate credit risk before approving loans.

Traditional manual assessments can be time-consuming and inconsistent, making it difficult to identify borrowers with a higher likelihood of default.

Using predictive models improves the consistency and efficiency of credit risk assessment.

---

## Objective

Develop a classification model capable of:

- Predicting probability of default
- Supporting lending decisions
- Improving credit risk assessment
- Identifying high-risk borrowers

---

## Dataset

The model uses historical borrower information including financial and demographic characteristics together with loan repayment outcomes.

Variables include:

- Applicant Income
- Loan Amount
- Credit History
- Employment Information
- Loan Status

---

## Methodology

### Data Preparation

- Data Cleaning
- Missing Value Treatment
- Feature Engineering

### Model Development

Developed a Logistic Regression model to classify borrowers into default and non-default categories.

### Model Evaluation

Evaluated model performance using:

- ROC Curve
- ROC-AUC
- Confusion Matrix
- Classification Accuracy

---

## Results

The model successfully identifies borrowers with a higher probability of default and demonstrates how predictive analytics can support more informed lending decisions.

---

## Business Value

This project demonstrates how financial institutions use data-driven models to:

- Improve lending quality
- Reduce credit losses
- Support risk management
- Strengthen credit approval processes
